Audit Context

Audit: Billing Process — Sale of Heavy Equipment Parts and Components

Company: MaquiParts Industrial S.A. — Regional leader in the distribution of spare parts and components for heavy machinery in the mining and construction sectors.

Audit Period: January 2025 – December 2025

Context: During the audit period, the company processed 12,450 invoices for the sale of parts and components totaling USD $47.8 million. It was identified that 2,118 invoices (17%) were rejected by customers, generating rework, collection delays, and potential impacts on commercial relationships.

Main reported rejection causes:

  • Discrepancies between quoted and billed prices (38% of rejections)
  • Errors in part numbers or component descriptions (25%)
  • Expired or unreferenced purchase orders (22%)
  • Incomplete or missing shipping documentation (15%)

Level 2 — Risk Rating Assignment

Assign a risk level (Grade) to each finding based on the definitions in Appendix A – Grade Definitions for Observations.

Appendix A – Grade Definitions for Observations

HIGH
Observations to be addressed by management as a matter of urgency.
  1. Involve or may lead to significant misstatement of the audited entity's assets, liabilities, revenue and expenses; and/or
  2. Observations or weakness fundamental and material to the system / control; and/or
  3. Could significantly impact operating efficiency and/or effectiveness; and/or
  4. Likely will cause control objective not being met.
  5. Non-compliance with group policies without any mitigating control.
MEDIUM
Observations that do not require immediate action but should be addressed in near term.
  1. Involve or may lead to moderate misstatement of the audited entity's assets, liabilities, revenue, and expenses; and/or
  2. Observations or weakness has important effect on the system or control; and/or
  3. Moderately impact operating efficiency and/or effectiveness.
  4. A system objective may still be met in full or in part with mitigating controls; and/or
  5. Non-compliance with group policies with mitigating controls.
LOW
Observations that do not appear to have a substantially negative impact on operations or the control environment.
  1. Involve or may lead to minor misstatement of the audited entity's assets, liabilities, revenue, and expenses; and/or
  2. Would, if corrected, improve the internal control but are not vital to the overall system of internal control; and/or
  3. Minimally impact operational efficiency and effectiveness.
